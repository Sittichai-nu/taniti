Travel Tips & FAQs

Click any question to expand the answer.

► What currency is accepted on Taniti?
Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its primary currency. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and most businesses accept major credit cards.
► What is the legal drinking age on Taniti?
The legal drinking age on Taniti is 18. Note that this law is not strictly enforced. Alcohol may not be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m.
► Is Taniti safe for tourists?
Violent crime is very rare on Taniti. However, as tourism has increased there are more reports of pickpocketing and petty crimes. Visitors should take standard precautions with valuables in busy tourist areas.
► What language is spoken on the island?
Taniti has its own indigenous language. Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English in Taniti City and Merriton Landing. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by older residents.
► Are there medical facilities available on the island?
Yes. Taniti has one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees, making it accessible to international visitors. Travel health insurance is recommended before arriving.
► What type of power outlets does Taniti use?
Power outlets on Taniti are 120 volts — the same standard used in the United States. American travelers do not need a voltage converter or plug adapter.
► Which holidays affect business hours?
Taniti observes a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on these days. Visitors are strongly encouraged to check the holiday calendar when planning their itinerary.
► How do I get from the airport to my hotel?
Options from the airport include rental cars from a local agency near the airport, taxis available in Taniti City, hotel shuttles (check with your accommodation), and public buses running 5:00 a.m. to 11:00 p.m. daily within Taniti City.
